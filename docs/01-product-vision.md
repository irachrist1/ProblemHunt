# Product Vision

## The Core Insight

**Product Hunt** answers: *"What was built?"*  
**ProblemHunt** answers: *"What needs to be built?"*

The gap between real problems and what gets built exists because builders optimize for what's visible, not what's painful. An upvoted, clearly-defined problem is de-risked demand signal — essentially free market research that removes guesswork from what to build next.

---

## Elevator Pitch

> ProblemHunt is a community platform where people share, upvote, and track real-world problems that deserve solutions. Organizations use it internally to align teams on what to build. Builders use it publicly to find what's worth building next.

---

## The Problem We're Solving

1. **Builders build the wrong things** — because they don't know what's actually painful enough to pay for
2. **Organizations lack a structured way to surface problems** — Slack threads, Notion docs, and Jira backlogs lose the signal
3. **Great ideas die in isolation** — a developer has a painful problem but doesn't know 10,000 others share it
4. **Side projects lack direction** — "what should I build?" is one of the most Googled developer questions
5. **Demand validation is broken** — surveys are slow, fake, and easy to game. Upvoted problems are real.

## Who This Is For

### Primary Users

| User Type | Context | Core Need |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| **Product Managers** | Company/startup | Know which user problems deserve the next sprint |
| **Developers** | Side projects / open source | Find problems worth solving before spending months building |
| **Founders** | Pre-product | Validate that a problem space has real, quantifiable demand |
| **Designers** | Agency / in-house | Understand context and pain before designing solutions |
| **Individuals** | Daily life / work | Vent frustrations, find others with the same problem |

### Secondary Users
- **Investors** — evaluating problem spaces and unmet demand
- **Researchers** — studying patterns in human pain points
- **Journalists** — identifying trends in consumer/enterprise friction
- **Students** — finding real capstone or thesis project ideas

---

## Two Modes of Use

### 1. Public Community (Growth Engine)
Open, Product Hunt-style feed. Anyone posts problems. Anyone votes. Problems gain credibility through community validation. The public layer drives discovery, SEO, and word-of-mouth.

### 2. Private Workspaces (Revenue Engine)
Organizations create private boards. Internal teams post and vote on problems. Leadership uses the data to prioritize roadmaps. Integrates with Linear, Jira, Notion. This is the B2B SaaS play.

Both modes are deeply interconnected — private problems can be made public, public problems can be imported to workspaces.

## Why This Will Succeed

### Structural Advantages
- **Network effects**: More problems → more votes → more signal → more builders → more solutions → more people posting
- **Compounding data**: Every problem + outcome teaches the AI what patterns matter
- **Dual flywheel**: Public community feeds B2B pipeline; B2B users seed quality content for community

### Timing
- AI makes problem analysis (deduplication, clarity scoring, solution matching) finally practical
- Remote-first culture means async prioritization tools have replaced in-person meetings
- Developer-led growth is ascendant — tools that win with devs expand from there

### Moat
- **Structured problem data** — proprietary dataset of validated problems with demand signals
- **Problem-to-solution graph** — linking problems to their solutions over time creates unique insight
- **Community trust** — first-mover advantage in "problems as first-class objects"

## Success Metrics

### North Star Metric
**Weekly Active Problem Posters (WAPP)** — the number of users who post at least one problem per week. This captures both supply (content creation) and intent (active engagement).

### Supporting Metrics

| Metric | Target (6mo) | Target (18mo) |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Total problems posted | 10,000 | 500,000 |
| Average votes per problem | 12 | 45 |
| % problems with ≥1 solution | 20% | 55% |
| Workspace monthly active orgs | 50 | 2,000 |
| Net Promoter Score | 45+ | 60+ |
| Time-to-first-vote (median) | < 30 min | < 15 min |

## What ProblemHunt Is NOT

- **Not a bug tracker** — for user-facing product problems, not internal code issues
- **Not a feature request board** — problems must be stated as problems, not solutions ("I want X" is not allowed; "I struggle with Y because Z" is)
- **Not a forum** — discussion is secondary; structured data is primary
- **Not another Twitter/Reddit** — no hot takes, no memes, no engagement-bait. Signal only.

---

## The Long Game

**Year 1**: Community of 100K+ engaged problem-posters and solution-seekers. 200+ paying organizations using workspaces.

**Year 2**: The definitive dataset of unmet human needs. AI-powered matching connects problems to builders automatically. Bounty marketplace launches.

**Year 3**: Becomes the first place every serious builder checks before starting a new project. Integrated into the decision-making workflow of every serious product team.

## Name Decision (Required Before Launch)

| Candidate | Pros | Cons |
|-----------|------|------|
| **Signal** | Clean, minimal, evokes data/insight | Generic, taken in many domains |
| **Friction** | Honest, visceral, memorable | Negative connotation |
| **ProblemHunt** | Descriptive, Product Hunt familiarity | Derivative, long |
| **Unmet** | Elegant, implies unmet needs | Abstract |
| **Forge** | Implies creation from raw material | Solution-focused, not problem-focused |
| **Ache** | Human, emotional | Too medical/personal |

**Recommendation**: **Signal** — short, premium-feeling, brand-ready, works as both noun and verb.
